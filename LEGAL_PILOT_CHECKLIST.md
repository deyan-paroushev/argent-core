# Legal pilot checklist

**Purpose:** define legal questions and conditions precedent for an institutional pilot.  
**Status:** issue checklist, not legal advice and not a legal opinion.

## Non-negotiable boundary

Soroban records do not, by themselves, create, perfect, register, prioritize, or enforce a security interest in physical bullion.

The legal effect comes from applicable law, executed agreements, party capacity and authority, custody and control arrangements, any required registration or possession, and the enforcement and insolvency regime. The protocol may preserve evidence of those acts; it does not replace them.

No live collateral should enter the pilot until UAE counsel, bank counsel, and any relevant custody-jurisdiction counsel have answered the checklist below and the bank has accepted the result.

## Official UAE legal starting points

The following are starting points for counsel, not conclusions about Argent's structure:

| Official source | Relevance |
|---|---|
| [Federal Law No. 4 of 2020 Regarding Securing the Rights in Movables](https://uaelegislation.gov.ae/en/legislations/1446) | The official UAE legislation portal lists the law as active, issued 28 May 2020 and effective 29 May 2020. It defines a framework for security rights in movables. Counsel must determine its application to the chosen bullion, custody, parties, and secured obligations. |
| [Cabinet Resolution No. 29 of 2021](https://uaelegislation.gov.ae/en/legislations/1487) | The active Executive Regulations address the register, registration information, searches, amendments, priority-related matters, and execution procedures. Counsel must determine the required filing and asset description. |
| [Federal Decree-Law No. 51 of 2023 Promulgating the Financial and Bankruptcy Law](https://uaelegislation.gov.ae/en/legislations/2190) | The official portal lists this law as active and effective 1 May 2024. Counsel must analyze stays, secured-creditor treatment, avoidance, asset control, and enforcement in an applicant insolvency. |

The official portal warns that the English text is a translation and the Arabic text prevails in interpretation and application. Counsel should work from the authoritative text and all current amendments and implementing decisions.

## 1. Jurisdiction and legal characterization

- Identify the applicant's place of incorporation, principal business, and insolvency forum.
- Identify the bank entity, branch, regulator, and product-booking location.
- Identify the custodian entity, vault location, governing custody law, and any sub-custodian.
- Confirm whether the structure is mainland UAE, a financial free zone, another free zone, or cross-border.
- Characterize the customer's interest: direct title to allocated bars, co-ownership, contractual entitlement, bailment, deposit, warehouse interest, or another right.
- Confirm whether the bars are goods, inventory, financial collateral, or another legal category for each relevant rule.
- Determine conflict-of-laws rules for tangible bullion, contractual rights, registration, and enforcement.

**Required output:** jurisdiction and characterization memorandum with no unresolved assumption material to enforceability.

## 2. Ownership and asset eligibility

- Verify legal and beneficial ownership of each bar.
- Exclude customer assets, consigned metal, leased metal, trust assets, unallocated claims, and assets subject to retention-of-title or competing rights unless counsel expressly approves them.
- Confirm transfer history, invoices, refinery and assay evidence, custody statements, insurance, and location.
- Define the legally sufficient description of present and substituted bars.
- Confirm the applicant has corporate power and authority to encumber the metal.
- Determine whether acquisition shortly before the pledge creates avoidance, preference, or source-of-funds concerns.

**Required output:** title and eligibility opinion or agreed diligence standard, with an exception process.

## 3. Security agreement and secured obligations

- Identify pledgor, secured party, security agent, control agent, guarantor, and beneficiary roles.
- Define the secured obligations: reimbursement, fees, indemnities, enforcement costs, FX amounts, and contingent liabilities.
- Confirm whether future, amended, renewed, or multiple obligations are covered.
- Define attachment or creation requirements and the moment the security becomes effective between the parties.
- Describe collateral, proceeds, substitutions, insurance proceeds, and realized value with legally sufficient precision.
- Align negative pledge, disposal, further-encumbrance, top-up, substitution, release, and margin provisions.
- Ensure protocol states do not conflict with the agreements or imply rights that the documents do not create.

**Required output:** executed security package and a state-to-document mapping approved by bank counsel.

## 4. Custodian control

- Determine whether possession, control, acknowledgement, account blocking, registration, or a combination is required.
- Obtain the custodian's acknowledgement of the bank's security and control instructions.
- Define who may instruct movement, substitution, release, sale, assay, transport, or withdrawal.
- Prevent unilateral applicant disposition and undisclosed further encumbrance.
- Address commingling, segregation, bar substitution, vault transfer, sub-custody, liens, set-off, and custodian insolvency.
- Establish discrepancy, loss, fraud, insurance, force-majeure, and business-continuity procedures.
- Confirm the custodian's liability standard and the bank's access to records and inspection.

**Required output:** control agreement and custodian operating procedure tested against each protocol transition.

## 5. Perfection, registration, and priority

- Determine whether Federal Law No. 4 of 2020 applies to the exact structure.
- Determine whether registration, possession, control, notice, or another act makes the right effective against third parties.
- Identify the correct registry, registrant, debtor identifier, collateral description, validity period, and amendment process.
- Complete searches for prior security rights, liens, attachment orders, title claims, and custodian rights.
- Establish priority against later secured creditors, judgment creditors, purchasers, insolvency officeholders, and custodian claimants.
- Define renewal, amendment, continuation, substitution, release, and cancellation procedures.
- For multi-bank expansion, define intercreditor, security-agent, sharing, and enforcement-control terms before any shared allocation.

**Required output:** filing or perfection opinion, registry evidence, search report, priority analysis, and renewal calendar.

## 6. Bank instrument and reimbursement

- Confirm the bank's regulatory and internal authority to issue the chosen instrument.
- Confirm beneficiary acceptance, form, governing rules, claim channel, expiry, cancellation, and amendment mechanics.
- Ensure the bank instrument remains independent or accessory as applicable and is not accidentally made conditional on Argent state.
- Define when contingent exposure crystallizes and when reimbursement becomes due.
- Align currency conversion, interest or fees, partial claims, multiple drawings, and disputed claims.
- Confirm that Argent does not make the claim decision or become the issuer.

**Required output:** approved instrument form, facility agreement, reimbursement terms, and product-system authority map.

## 7. Insolvency and enforcement

- Analyze the effect of applicant insolvency on control, registration, stays, set-off, collateral proceeds, and enforcement timing.
- Analyze custodian and bank insolvency or resolution scenarios.
- Determine whether pre-insolvency grants, substitutions, top-ups, or releases are vulnerable to avoidance.
- Define notice, valuation, possession, sale, appropriation, court involvement, and proceeds-distribution requirements.
- Define who controls enforcement and how the protocol records—but does not decide—the authorized outcome.
- Confirm treatment of surplus, shortfall, expenses, taxes, and currency conversion.
- Rehearse contested title, injunction, registry error, custodian refusal, and conflicting order scenarios.

**Required output:** enforceability and insolvency memorandum plus a bank-approved enforcement playbook.

## 8. Regulatory, compliance, data, and evidence

- Confirm that Argent's activities do not require an unplanned banking, custody, lending, guarantee, commodity, brokerage, payment, virtual-asset, or financial-services licence.
- Map AML, sanctions, source-of-funds, responsible-sourcing, anti-bribery, and trade-control responsibilities.
- Determine data-controller, processor, localization, retention, secrecy, outsourcing, audit, and cross-border transfer obligations.
- Approve institutional electronic signatures, records, timestamps, hashes, and Soroban anchors as evidence, while preserving authoritative originals.
- Define which records require Arabic form, notarization, legalization, or original execution.
- Complete tax, accounting, prudential, capital, provisioning, and financial-reporting analysis.
- Confirm insurance coverage and loss-payee arrangements.

**Required output:** regulatory perimeter memo, compliance responsibility matrix, data-processing package, and evidence policy.

## 9. Conditions precedent to live collateral

All items below must be marked complete by named institutional owners:

- final legal structure and governing law;
- ownership and eligible-bar verification;
- executed facility, security, custody-control, and data agreements;
- required registration or other perfection completed and independently checked;
- priority and insolvency opinions accepted by the bank;
- beneficiary instrument form accepted;
- bank credit, legal, compliance, operational-risk, technology, and product approvals;
- custodian legal, operations, security, and insurance approvals;
- company board and signatory authority;
- protocol authority matrix matched to legal signatories;
- privacy, security, recovery, reconciliation, and enforcement exercises passed;
- pilot limits, stop conditions, incident escalation, and unwind plan approved.

## Legal-to-protocol rule

Every legally meaningful protocol state must have:

1. an authoritative off-chain act or document;
2. an identified actor with legal and system authority;
3. a defined effective time;
4. evidence and retention requirements;
5. a correction and dispute process;
6. a rule for inconsistency between the legal record, bank system, custodian record, private Argent state, and public anchor.

Where records conflict, the protocol must enter a safe exception state. It must not infer that the blockchain record automatically prevails.
